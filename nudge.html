Nudge iOS App

2026

The best product advice I received was simple: solve a problem you actually live with.

"The most valuable skill of the 21st century is knowing what to do with AI." Pedro Domingos

A football grounds tracker that never shipped taught me everything, where I cut corners, what I'd do differently, how to think about architecture before writing a single line. Nudge is where those lessons got applied.

Beautiful interfaces. Zero infrastructure. Everything into the frontend, nothing behind the door. Nudge was built differently.

Backend first, frontend later. The unglamorous stuff, databases, auth flows, relational structure, the plumbing nobody sees but everything depends on.

The backend is the 2026 equivalent of a receptionist. Nobody notices it when it's working. Everyone notices it when it's not.

Building an iOS app isn't designing a few screens. It's designing every screen, every state, every decision a user could possibly make. Layer after layer after layer, each one revealing another underneath.

Joining a team, sending a nudge, handling edge cases. None of it appears by accident. The cleaner something feels to use, the more invisible work went into it. This website is a good example of that.

Started with Lovable, then Replit. Useful starting points, but there's a ceiling. Moved to Cursor when the friction became the signal.

Gemini for UI and visual thinking. Claude for backend logic. The right model for the right job. That knowledge compounds in a way generic AI literacy simply doesn't.
